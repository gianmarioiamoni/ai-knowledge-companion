# Advanced English Grammar and Writing

## Chapter 1: Complex Sentence Structures

### 1.1 Sentence Types

**Simple Sentence**: One independent clause
- Example: "The cat sleeps."

**Compound Sentence**: Two or more independent clauses joined by coordinating conjunctions (FANBOYS: For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So)
- Example: "The cat sleeps, and the dog barks."

**Complex Sentence**: One independent clause + one or more dependent clauses
- Example: "When the sun sets, the cat sleeps."

**Compound-Complex Sentence**: Two or more independent clauses + one or more dependent clauses
- Example: "When the sun sets, the cat sleeps, and the dog barks."

### 1.2 Dependent Clauses

**Adjective Clauses** (Relative Clauses): Modify nouns
- Relative pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, that
- Example: "The book *that I read* was fascinating."

**Adverb Clauses**: Modify verbs, adjectives, or adverbs
- Subordinating conjunctions: when, while, because, although, if, unless, since
- Example: "*Because it was raining*, we stayed inside."

**Noun Clauses**: Function as nouns
- Example: "*What she said* surprised everyone."

### 1.3 Parallel Structure

Items in a series should have the same grammatical form:

❌ Incorrect: "She likes hiking, to swim, and riding bikes."
✅ Correct: "She likes hiking, swimming, and biking."

❌ Incorrect: "The manager wants employees who are punctual, hard-working, and have creativity."
✅ Correct: "The manager wants employees who are punctual, hard-working, and creative."

## Chapter 2: Advanced Verb Forms

### 2.1 Perfect Tenses

**Present Perfect**: Action started in past, relevant to present
- Form: have/has + past participle
- Example: "I have lived here for five years." (Still living here)

**Past Perfect**: Action completed before another past action
- Form: had + past participle
- Example: "She had finished dinner before the guests arrived."

**Future Perfect**: Action will be completed before a future time
- Form: will have + past participle
- Example: "By next year, I will have graduated."

### 2.2 Perfect Continuous Tenses

**Present Perfect Continuous**: Ongoing action from past to present
- Form: have/has been + verb-ing
- Example: "I have been studying for three hours."

**Past Perfect Continuous**: Ongoing action before another past action
- Form: had been + verb-ing
- Example: "She had been working there for ten years when she got promoted."

**Future Perfect Continuous**: Ongoing action until a future time
- Form: will have been + verb-ing
- Example: "By December, I will have been living here for a decade."

### 2.3 Modal Verbs

**Ability**: can, could, be able to
- Present: "She can speak Spanish."
- Past: "She could swim when she was five."
- Future: "She will be able to attend."

**Possibility/Probability**:
- may, might (50% probability)
- could (possibility)
- must (strong probability, deduction)
- Example: "It must be raining." (I'm almost certain)

**Obligation/Necessity**:
- must, have to (strong obligation)
- should, ought to (advice/recommendation)
- Example: "You must submit the form by Friday."

**Permission**: can, may, could
- Formal: "May I use your phone?"
- Informal: "Can I borrow your pen?"

## Chapter 3: Conditional Sentences

### 3.1 Types of Conditionals

**Zero Conditional**: General truths, scientific facts
- Form: If + present simple, present simple
- Example: "If you heat water to 100°C, it boils."

**First Conditional**: Real possibility in future
- Form: If + present simple, will + base verb
- Example: "If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home."

**Second Conditional**: Hypothetical/unlikely present or future
- Form: If + past simple, would + base verb
- Example: "If I won the lottery, I would travel the world."

**Third Conditional**: Hypothetical past (impossible to change)
- Form: If + past perfect, would have + past participle
- Example: "If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam."

### 3.2 Mixed Conditionals

**Past action, present result**:
- Form: If + past perfect, would + base verb
- Example: "If I had saved money, I would be rich now."

**Present situation, past result**:
- Form: If + past simple, would have + past participle
- Example: "If I were more careful, I wouldn't have broken it."

### 3.3 Other Conditional Forms

**Using "unless"** (if not):
- "I won't go unless you come with me."
- = "I won't go if you don't come with me."

**Using "provided/providing that", "as long as"**:
- "You can stay, provided that you're quiet."

**Using "in case"** (for precaution):
- "Take an umbrella in case it rains."

## Chapter 4: Reported Speech

### 4.1 Statement Reporting

**Direct Speech**: "I am tired," she said.
**Reported Speech**: She said (that) she was tired.

**Tense Changes (Backshift)**:
- Present simple → Past simple
- Present continuous → Past continuous
- Present perfect → Past perfect
- Past simple → Past perfect
- Will → Would
- Can → Could

**Time and Place Changes**:
- now → then
- today → that day
- tomorrow → the next day
- yesterday → the previous day
- here → there
- this → that

### 4.2 Question Reporting

**Yes/No Questions**:
- Direct: "Are you coming?" he asked.
- Reported: He asked if/whether I was coming.

**Wh- Questions**:
- Direct: "Where do you live?" she asked.
- Reported: She asked where I lived.

**Note**: Word order changes to statement order (no inversion)

### 4.3 Command and Request Reporting

**Commands**:
- Direct: "Close the door!" she said.
- Reported: She told me to close the door.

**Requests**:
- Direct: "Can you help me?" he asked.
- Reported: He asked me to help him.

**Negative Commands**:
- Direct: "Don't be late!" she said.
- Reported: She told me not to be late.

## Chapter 5: Advanced Writing Techniques

### 5.1 Cohesion and Coherence

**Cohesive Devices**:

**Addition**: furthermore, moreover, in addition, additionally
- "The course is challenging. Moreover, it requires dedication."

**Contrast**: however, nevertheless, nonetheless, on the other hand
- "The weather was cold. Nevertheless, we enjoyed the trip."

**Cause and Effect**: therefore, consequently, as a result, thus
- "He studied hard. Consequently, he passed the exam."

**Example**: for instance, for example, such as, namely
- "Many cities are polluted, for instance, Beijing and Delhi."

**Time Sequence**: meanwhile, subsequently, eventually, finally
- "First, we researched. Subsequently, we drafted the report."

### 5.2 Academic Writing Style

**Formal vs. Informal**:

Avoid contractions:
- ❌ "They're" → ✅ "They are"

Avoid phrasal verbs (use single-word equivalents):
- ❌ "put off" → ✅ "postpone"
- ❌ "find out" → ✅ "discover"
- ❌ "go up" → ✅ "increase"

Avoid personal pronouns in formal writing:
- ❌ "I think that..." → ✅ "It can be argued that..."
- ❌ "You can see that..." → ✅ "It is evident that..."

Use passive voice when appropriate:
- ❌ "The researchers conducted the study..." 
- ✅ "The study was conducted..."

### 5.3 Essay Structure

**Introduction**:
- Hook: Engage the reader
- Background: Provide context
- Thesis statement: Main argument/position

**Body Paragraphs** (PEEL structure):
- **Point**: Topic sentence
- **Evidence**: Supporting facts, examples, quotes
- **Explanation**: Analysis of evidence
- **Link**: Connection to thesis or transition to next paragraph

**Conclusion**:
- Restate thesis (in different words)
- Summarize main points
- Final thought or call to action

### 5.4 Common Writing Errors

**Subject-Verb Agreement**:
- ❌ "The list of items are long."
- ✅ "The list of items is long."
- (Subject is "list", not "items")

**Dangling Modifiers**:
- ❌ "Walking down the street, the trees were beautiful."
- ✅ "Walking down the street, I saw beautiful trees."

**Comma Splices**:
- ❌ "It was raining, we stayed inside."
- ✅ "It was raining, so we stayed inside."
- ✅ "It was raining; we stayed inside."

**Fragment Sentences**:
- ❌ "Because it was raining."
- ✅ "We stayed inside because it was raining."

**Apostrophe Misuse**:
- ❌ "The dog wagged it's tail." (it's = it is)
- ✅ "The dog wagged its tail." (possessive)

**Commonly Confused Words**:
- affect (verb) vs. effect (noun)
- than (comparison) vs. then (time)
- lose (verb) vs. loose (adjective)
- their (possessive) vs. there (place) vs. they're (they are)

## Chapter 6: Idioms and Expressions

### 6.1 Common Idioms

**Time**:
- "In the nick of time" - just in time
- "Once in a blue moon" - very rarely
- "Around the clock" - 24 hours a day

**Money**:
- "Cost an arm and a leg" - very expensive
- "Break the bank" - spend all your money
- "Penny for your thoughts" - asking what someone is thinking

**Success/Failure**:
- "Hit the nail on the head" - exactly right
- "Back to square one" - start over
- "Break a leg" - good luck

**Emotions**:
- "Under the weather" - feeling ill
- "On cloud nine" - very happy
- "Down in the dumps" - sad, depressed

### 6.2 Phrasal Verbs

**Separable**:
- "turn off" - "Turn off the light" or "Turn the light off"
- "put on" - "Put on your jacket" or "Put your jacket on"

**Inseparable**:
- "look after" - "Look after the children" (not "Look the children after")
- "run into" - "I ran into an old friend"

**Common Phrasal Verbs**:
- "give up" - quit, stop trying
- "look forward to" - anticipate with pleasure
- "take off" - remove clothing; airplane leaving ground
- "work out" - exercise; find a solution
- "show up" - appear, arrive

### 6.3 Collocations

**Strong Collocations**:
- "fast food" (not quick food)
- "heavy rain" (not strong rain)
- "make a decision" (not do a decision)
- "take a photo" (not make a photo)

**Verb + Noun**:
- make: a mistake, progress, money, an effort
- do: homework, business, damage, the dishes
- have: a shower, a conversation, a problem
- take: a break, a risk, time, a seat

**Advanced**:
